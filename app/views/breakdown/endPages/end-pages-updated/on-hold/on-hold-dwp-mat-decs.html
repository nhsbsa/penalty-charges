Challenge a Penalty Charge Notice

You’ve told us that you were pregnant at the time you claimed free or reduced cost dental treatment, or had given birth in the previous 12 months

If you were pregnant at the time or had given birth in the previous 12 months, or were on income-related Employment and Support Allowance you were entitled to free or reduced cost dental treatment.

What to do next

You have the following options:

  • Send proof that you were pregnant, or had given birth in the previous 12 months at the time you had claimed free or reduced cost dental treatment. If you send us valid proof, you won’t have to pay anything.
  • Send proof that you were named on a claim for Income Support or income-related Employment and Support Allowance at the time you claimed the free or reduced cost dental treatment. If you send us valid proof, you won’t have to pay anything.
  • Pay the total amount of £168.60.

If you were not entitled to free or reduced cost dental treatment but have an exceptional reason why you made the claim, you can ask us to review your Penalty Charge Notice. Find out if you can ask for a review.
